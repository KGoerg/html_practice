I Love My Family

Thankfulness

I want to spend some describing the members of my family and reflecting on why I'm thankful for each of them. It's important to stop, reflect, and be grateful. We've had a lot of ups and downs at the start of 2025, but now things are peaceful, so there's plenty to be thankful for!

I'm very fortunate to have a loving family consisting of my husband, Aaron, and my two cats, Gaia and Hot Sauce.

Aaron

Aaron is a wonderful person who makes me laugh every day and makes me feel so loved. I've known him since 2015, when we first met at Illinois State University shortly before our freshman year ended. I thought he seemed nice and I could tell he was talented -- he was making his own music and showed off his creativity during our Dungeons & Dragons session, which is where I first laid eyes on him. I knew I wanted to get to know him better, and coincidentally, he grew up in a city half an hour away from where I grew up, and we were heading to our parents' homes for the summer, so we were able to see each other very easily for a couple of months before we started back at ISU! We dated through the rest of college and have been together ever since, eventually getting married on November 8th, 2024. We're coming up on our one year marriage anniversary soon, which is exciting!

Aaron is my other half and keeps me balanced. I'm so grateful to love someone so inspiring and encouraging, who understands me, and to have someone that makes life feel so much easier. He's a great cat-dad and it warms my heart every day to see how much love he has to give our little babies, and to me. If you're reading this, my love, I hope you know you're the best thing to happen to me and I'm so proud of the life we've built together so far.

Gaia & Hot Sauce

Gaia

Gaia and Hot Sauce are our two one-and-a-half year old cats who keep me on my toes and help make our household a joy to live in! They were born in early March of 2024 and were adopted by Aaron and I on August 8, 2024. As of writing this blog, tomorrow is actually their one year adoption day!

Gaia has arguably changed the most since we adopted her. After keeping her and her brother locked in our bathroom for a week to decompress and to ensure no signs of ringworm were visible (long story), Gaia started showing signs of illness. The vet found she had a fever and, after some fluids and monitoring, we brought her home.

The same thing happened about a month later. However, after fluids were administered and we brought her home, she displayed anisocoria, which meant her pupils were two different sizes. After a lot of back and forth with her regular vet and an opthalmologist over the span of a few months, we learned Gaia had Feline Leukemia Virus, also known as FeLV. FeLV weakens a cat's immune system, which means the eye infections and inflammation her opthalmologist identified were more difficult than usual to treat.

Eventually, the inflammation won, and both of her eyes were removed in February of 2024, but she is now happier than ever! We also learned in July 2024 that her FeLV is regressive, which means her immune system is keeping up with the virus and she's not contagious, nor is she as likely to develop serious illnesses due to the virus!

Gaia is an incredibly silly little girl who loves to snuggle with me and give me what we lovingly call leaping huggies, where instead of rubbing against my legs like cats usually do, she also does a little jump up against my legs. She has started waiting for me at the door when I come home, and sometimes I can hear her squeaking at the door when I leave, as if she's saying, "Come back! Don't go!!". She's a little love bug with a wild side, which her brother can definitely attest to -- even though she's blind, she likes to chase and pounce on him.
